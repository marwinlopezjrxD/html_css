Chainsaw Man is the only manga that will grab your soul, rip it out with a chainsaw, feed it to dogs, and somehow make you thank it for the privilege — Denji’s raw, unfiltered struggle for basic human affection hits harder than any shounen power fantasy, and every chapter is pure chaotic genius that breaks every rule while still making you cry, laugh, and scream in 19 pages.

Fujimoto doesn’t care about your feelings: he’ll give you the greatest love confession in anime history, the most insane fights ever drawn, and then immediately punch you in the gut with twists you never saw coming — Reze, Quanxi, the Gun Devil, the Falling Devil, all peak fiction dropped like bombs, while the art flips from cute to horrifying to god-tier in a single panel.

If you skip Chainsaw Man, you’re choosing to live a weaker, less explosive life — this series will ruin all other manga for you, make you join cult-like CSM Discords at 3 AM, and leave you broken screaming “WHERE IS PART 2”. Read it. Suffer beautifully. Reze deserves your tears.

In short, CSM is pain.
